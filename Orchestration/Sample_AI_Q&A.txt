Artificial Intelligence (AI): The simulation of human intelligence in machines that are capable of learning, reasoning, problem-solving, and decision-making.

Machine Learning (ML): A subset of AI that involves the development of algorithms and statistical models that enable computers to learn and improve from experience without being explicitly programmed.

Deep Learning: A subfield of machine learning that utilizes artificial neural networks to model and learn complex patterns and representations from data.

Neural Networks: Computational models inspired by the human brain's neural structure that can be used for tasks such as pattern recognition, classification, and regression.

Data-driven: An approach that emphasizes the use of data to guide decision-making and problem-solving processes.

Cognitive Computing: An AI system that attempts to mimic human cognitive abilities, such as learning, reasoning, understanding, and interacting with natural language.

Natural Language Processing (NLP): A branch of AI that focuses on enabling computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language.

Computer Vision: A field of AI that involves teaching computers to interpret and understand visual information from images and videos.

Predictive Analytics: The use of historical data and statistical techniques to make predictions about future events or outcomes.

Big Data: Refers to large and complex datasets that are difficult to process and analyze using traditional data processing methods.

Algorithm: A step-by-step procedure or set of rules used to solve a specific problem or perform a task.

Automation: The use of technology and machines to perform tasks and processes without human intervention.

Robotic Process Automation (RPA): The use of software bots to automate repetitive and rule-based tasks traditionally performed by humans.

Intelligent Systems: Systems that use AI techniques to exhibit intelligent behavior and adapt to changing conditions.

Expert Systems: AI systems that emulate the decision-making ability of a human expert in a specific domain.

Smart Machines: Devices or systems that incorporate AI and are capable of making decisions and taking actions based on their environment and data.

Autonomous Agents: AI systems or robots that can act independently in an environment, making decisions and taking actions to achieve specific goals.

Chatbots: AI-powered conversational agents that can interact with users and respond to their queries in natural language.

Virtual Assistants: AI-powered applications or devices that provide assistance to users through voice or text-based interactions.

Augmented Intelligence: The concept of combining human intelligence with AI to enhance decision-making and problem-solving capabilities.

Decision Support Systems: Computer-based systems that assist humans in making decisions by providing relevant information and analysis.

Pattern Recognition: The process of identifying patterns or regularities in data.

Sentiment Analysis: A technique used to determine the sentiment or emotional tone expressed in text data.

Image Recognition: The ability of an AI system to identify and classify objects, people, or features within an image.

Speech Recognition: AI technology that converts spoken language into text or commands.

Recommendation Systems: AI algorithms that suggest personalized recommendations to users based on their preferences and behavior.

Data Mining: The process of discovering patterns, relationships, or insights from large datasets.

Knowledge Representation: Methods for storing and organizing knowledge in a format that can be processed and used by AI systems.
