How to use this page

Use the Golden Rectangle Lab sliders to adjust height and width. When Lock ratio to φ is on, the width automatically becomes W = φ·H. Turn the lock off to explore other ratios and watch the error change.

In Fibonacci Convergence, increase Max n to generate more Fibonacci pairs and see how F(n+1)/F(n) approaches φ.

Accessibility notes: all controls are keyboard operable, and numeric readouts accompany every visualization.

Definition

Three equivalent ways to recognize φ: an equation, a geometry ratio, and a Fibonacci limit.

The golden ratio φ is the positive solution to the equation x = 1 + 1/x. It is also the width-to-height ratio of a golden rectangle, and it appears as the limit of ratios of consecutive Fibonacci numbers.

φ ≈ 1.6180339887
1/φ ≈ 0.6180339887
φ² = φ + 1 ≈ 2.6180339887

Notes

Key identities (and why they matter).

From x = 1 + 1/x, multiply by x to get x² = x + 1. The positive solution is φ, and the identity φ² = φ + 1 is what makes the rectangle subdivision repeat.

In the Fibonacci sequence (1, 1, 2, 3, 5, …), the ratio of consecutive terms converges to φ. This page shows the ratio and its error directly, so you do not need to rely on visuals alone.

Keyboard tips

  • Tab to sliders and use arrow keys for small changes.
  • Toggle “Lock ratio to φ” to snap the rectangle to the golden ratio.
  • Increase “Max n” to extend the convergence table and chart.
